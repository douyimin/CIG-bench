Abstract

Subsurface imaging and interpretation serve as the essential link between observational data and geological models. They support quantitative analyses of structural evolution, reservoir formation processes, and multiphysics coupling, and they enhance the assessment and optimization of geological model uncertainty. In applied domains, subsurface interpretation has become a central technology for hydrocarbon exploration, geological storage site selection for CO₂, and risk identification of geological hazards.

This study examines 652 publications from the past decade and categorizes them into four major research directions: structural interpretation, geobody identification, seismic facies analysis, and attribute estimation. We conducted citation network analysis to characterize the intrinsic connections and knowledge flow patterns among these categories.

We introduce CIG-Bench, a benchmark suite consisting of deep learning baselines together with the corresponding datasets, enabling reproducible qualitative comparisons in terms of structural continuity, geometric consistency, geological plausibility, cross-scale stability, and interpretability.

Key Contributions

1

Comprehensive survey of 652 publications spanning four major subsurface interpretation directions, with citation network analysis revealing cross-domain knowledge flow and methodological evolution.

2

Systematic review of deep learning methods across structure, geobody, facies, and property tasks, with task-specific recommendations and development pathways.

3

Introduction of CIG-Bench, an open-source benchmark covering fault interpretation, geobody segmentation, RGT estimation, and property modeling with cross-survey transferable baselines.

Interpretation Tasks

🏗️

Structure

Faults, horizons, unconformities, and relative geologic time (RGT) estimation for constructing structural frameworks.

🪨

Geobody

3D segmentation of salt bodies, channels, karst cavities, and igneous intrusions with independent geometries.

📊

Facies

Classification of seismic units by amplitude, frequency, continuity, and stratification reflecting depositional environments.

🔬

Property

Inversion of impedance, velocity, porosity, and density from seismic data calibrated with well logs.
